# Sales Process Pathway: "Whale Hunting" (High-Revenue Resorts)

**Target Segment:** 52 Resorts/Hotels with > RM50mil Annual Revenue.
**Strategy Type:** Account-Based Marketing (ABM).
**Goal:** High-value enterprise contracts (ACV > RM100k).

---

## üö´ What NOT To Do (The "List Burner")
- **Do NOT** send generic "Dear Sir/Madam" email blasts.
- **Do NOT** cold call the front desk asking for the "Manager".
- **Do NOT** lead with "We have a great dashboard software".
- *Why?* These hotels receive 50+ vendor pitches a day. You will be blocked immediately.

## üü¢ The 4-Phase Winning Pathway

### Phase 1: Intel Gathering ("The Dossier")
*Before a single message is sent, you must know more about their hotel than they expect.*

1.  **Map the Power Triangle:** Identify 3 key individuals via LinkedIn/Company Website.
    *   **The Check Signer:** General Manager (GM) or Owner.
    *   **The User:** Director of Revenue / Director of Sales & Marketing (DOSM).
    *   **The Gatekeeper/Enabler:** Executive Assistant (EA) or IT Director.
2.  **Tech Stack Recon:**
    *   Use tools like `BuiltWith` or check their booking engine URL (e.g., are they using SynXis, TravelClick, SiteMinder?).
    *   *Win Theme:* "We integrate perfectly with [Their Engine]."
3.  **Review Analysis:** Read their recent 1-star reviews on TripAdvisor/Google.
    *   *Pain Point Finding:* Look for "Slow service," "Long check-in," "Room not ready."
    *   *Pitch Ammo:* "I noticed guests complaining about wait times; our tool cuts check-in by 40%."

### Phase 2: The "Trojan Horse" Entry
*High-net-worth executives ignore email but open physical accounts.*

1.  **The "Lumpy Mail" Strategy (Direct Mail):**
    *   Send a FedEx/Courier package to the GM directly.
    *   **Contents:** A high-quality physical item (e.g., a leather notebook, a localized report) + A handwritten note.
    *   **The Note:** "Dear [Name], I analyzed [Resort Name]'s booking flow and found 3 bottlenecks losing you ~RM1.2m/year. Full report attached."
2.  **The "Insight" Connection (LinkedIn):**
    *   Connect with the Revenue Director.
    *   **Message:** "Hi [Name], wrote a short analysis on [Resort Name]'s ADR vs [Competitor] in the Langkawi market. Thought you'd find the pricing gap interesting. No pitch."
    *   *Goal:* Credibility, not a meeting (yet).

### Phase 3: The Value Proposition (Financial Language)
*Resort GMs care about **RevPAR** (Revenue Per Available Room) and **GOPPAR** (Gross Operating Profit Per Available Room).*

*   **Weak Pitch:** "Our software has a nice chat feature."
*   **Strong Pitch:** "Resort Genius captures the 15% of leads leaking from your current chat widget. For a 200-room resort like yours, that‚Äôs an estimated **RM 2.5mil in recovered revenue** annually."

**The "Math of Trust":**
> "We estimate your annual revenue at RM50mil. Market average marketing spend is 4% (RM2mil). If our tool improves conversion by just 1%, we pay for ourselves 10x over in Month 1."

### Phase 4: The Executive Briefing (The "Close")
*Never ask for a "Demo". Executives don't do demos; they do strategy.*

1.  **The Ask:** "I have a 15-minute Revenue Optimization Strategy deck specific to [Resort Name]. Do you have time next Tuesday?"
2.  **The Meeting:**
    *   Slide 1: **Their** Goals (Growth, ADR increase).
    *   Slide 2: **Their** Problems (Leaking leads, slow response).
    *   Slide 3: **The Fix** (Resort Genius).
    *   Slide 4: **The ROI** (RM value).
3.  **The Trial:** Offer a "Pilot Program" for one property if they are a group (e.g., Sunway). "Let us prove the 10x ROI on Property A before rolling out to the Group."
